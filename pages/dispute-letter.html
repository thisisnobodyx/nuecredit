Filing Instructions

  1. Copy the letter above and paste it into a word processor (Google Docs, Word, etc.).
  2. Replace all placeholder fields (your name, address, SSN last 4, etc.) with your real information.
  3. Print the letter, sign it by hand, and make a copy for your records.
  4. Mail the letter via USPS Certified Mail with Return Receipt Requested to the appropriate bureau address.
  5. The bureau has 30 days to investigate and respond under the FCRA. Keep your certified mail receipt as proof.

Tips for a Successful Dispute

  • Include copies (never originals) of any supporting documentation such as payment receipts, identity theft reports, or account statements.
  • Send separate letters for each item you are disputing — do not bundle multiple disputes into one letter.
  • Keep a detailed log of all correspondence dates, tracking numbers, and responses.
  • If the bureau does not respond within 30 days, the item must be removed from your report per FCRA Section 611.
  • Consider following up with the creditor directly (a “goodwill” or “debt validation” letter) for additional leverage.
